# AeroCommons Website Update — Owner-Produced Parts Pillar

## Context for Claude

AeroCommons is running a parallel project to the MAOS and Beech 215 prop controller work: reverse-engineering the **Beech P/N B200-202 pitch control bearing** for the Beech 215 Series electric controllable-pitch propeller, used on early Bonanza aircraft (35 through G35, 35R). The bearing has not been manufactured in decades, no production source exists, and failure grounds otherwise airworthy certificated aircraft.

The regulatory pathway for this project is **14 CFR 21.9(a)(5) — owner-produced parts** for certificated aircraft. This is completely distinct from the FAA Experimental Amateur-Built (EAB) framework that currently anchors the AeroCommons mission on the website.

The problem: the current site frames AeroCommons entirely around EAB. The bearing project — and the broader category of work it represents — is for **certificated legacy aircraft**, not experimental aircraft. These are different regulatory frameworks, different audiences, and different value propositions.

## What Needs to Be Added

Add a second distinct mission pillar to the AeroCommons website covering **legacy certificated aircraft preservation through owner-produced parts documentation**.

### Suggested language:

---

**Legacy Certificated Aircraft Preservation**

AeroCommons supports the reproduction of unavailable replacement parts for certificated legacy aircraft under **14 CFR 21.9(a)(5)**, the FAA's owner-produced parts provision.

For aircraft where critical components are no longer manufactured, we develop the engineering documentation — dimensional specifications, material callouts, and manufacturing drawings — that aircraft owners and their mechanics need to establish conformance to approved design and return airworthy aircraft to service.

This work is strictly non-commercial. Parts are produced by or for individual aircraft owners for installation on their own aircraft. AeroCommons provides the open technical documentation; the aircraft owner and certifying mechanic retain full responsibility for airworthiness determinations.

**Active project:** Beech P/N B200-202 pitch control bearing for the Beech 215 Series propeller — [github.com/billmallard/Beech215PropBearing](https://github.com/billmallard/Beech215PropBearing)

---

## Why This Matters for the Site

1. **Textron/Beechcraft outreach**: AeroCommons intends to contact Textron Aviation (Wichita) requesting release of original engineering drawings for B200-202 under a preservation/open-source license. If the site only references experimental aircraft, Textron's legal team will see a mission mismatch and likely dismiss or refuse the request. The site needs to reflect what AeroCommons actually does.

2. **Audience expansion**: A&Ps, type club members, and certificated aircraft owners do not self-identify with "experimental aircraft builders." This pillar makes AeroCommons legible to a large underserved community.

3. **Regulatory clarity**: Mixing EAB (14 CFR 21.191) and owner-produced parts (14 CFR 21.9(a)(5)) language, or implying they're the same, could confuse FAA contacts or industry partners. They should be presented as distinct programs.

4. **Organizational credibility**: Any formal outreach to manufacturers, type clubs (ABS, COPA, etc.), or the FAA on behalf of AeroCommons should be backed by a website that accurately describes this work.

## Tone Guidance

- Keep it non-commercial and safety-focused
- Signal regulatory literacy — use the CFR citations
- Do not position this as adversarial to manufacturers; Textron doesn't sell B200-202 and has no commercial interest in blocking this
- The framing is **preservation**, not disruption

## No Changes Needed To

- The EAB / experimental aircraft content — leave it as-is
- The MAOS project descriptions
- The Beech 215 prop controller project description (that one is already on the site)
- Core principles and values
